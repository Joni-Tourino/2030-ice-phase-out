Shell aims to install 50,000 on-street charging points by 2025

This would represent a third of the UK Committee for Climate Change recommendation

By Jonathan Touriño Jacobo - 02 September 2021
Credit: evclicks.co.uk

Shell have announced they aim on installing 50,000 on-street electric vehicle charging points by the end of 2025.

This would represent a third of the total on-street charging points recommended by the Climate Change Committee.

Last month, the Secretary of State for Transport, Grant Shapps, announced that the UK had reached 25,000 public EV chargepoints.

ubitricity, an on-street charger provider the oil company acquired in February of this year, is already the leader in the UK with almost three in every twenty chargers actually installed, according to Zap-Map.

The company also announced that they are willing to cover the cost of the remaining 25 % of installing charging posts for a Government grant, the On-Street Residential Charging Scheme (ORCS), to local authorities who are willing to install ubitricity’s.
