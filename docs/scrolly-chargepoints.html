The country needs to install more, and faster

Earlier this week the Secretary of State for Transport, Grant Shapps, announced that the UK reached 25,000 public EV chargepoints in the UK. He also announced that the Government was working on a chargepoint design as iconic as the red post box that is set to be unveiled at COP26 in November with a launch in 2022.

Where are they located?


Out of 374 local authorities, only the Isles of Scilly still lacks a public charging point. Westminster is leading the count with 1059 charging devices, followed by Wandsworth with 603 and Hammersmith and Fulham with 554. Ten local authorities still have less than ten charging devices. Back in October 2019, the earliest data sources available, less than a third (103) of local authorities had less than 10 charging points, with Barrow-in-Furness being the other one with none.

317 local authorities still have less than 100 public charging points. And 262 of them have less than one charging device for 10 electric vehicles. Pembrokeshire has the best rate in the UK with almost two vehicles per charging device.
