Yet, in the past days, Allegra Stratton (spokesperson for the COP26) and Michael Fabricant (Lichfield's MP) have both argued that they'd prefer to stay with their diesel vehicles arguing "range-anxiety" and the long times for charging an electric vehicle.

Where are they located?

Out of 374 local authorities, only the Isles of Scilly still lacks a public charging point. Westminster is leading the count with 1,059 charging devices, followed by Wandsworth with 603 and Hammersmith and Fulham with 554. Ten local authorities still have less than ten charging devices. Back in October 2019, the earliest data sources available, less than a third (103) of local authorities had less than 10 charging points, with Barrow-in-Furness being the other one with none.

Table of all the local authorities

317 local authorities still have less than 100 public charging points. And 262 of them have less than one charging device for 10 electric vehicles. Pembrokeshire has the best rate in the UK with almost two vehicles per charging device.

From the local authorities who have the most charging points in the UK, seven are in London, with Westminster in the lead with 1,059 charging points (the only LA passing the thousands), followed by Wandsworth, Hammersmith and Fulham. The first local authority outside of London is Coventry in the West Midlands, in fifth. The other two local authorities outside of London aare Milton Keynes and Brighton and Hove. For more information about other LAs you can search them in table below.

By vehicles licensed, the data collects all types indistinctively (BEV and PHEV), and they are allocated to a local authority according to the postcode they are registered. This also includes companies. This is why Stockport is the local authority with the most vehicles licensed with almost 35,000. According to Field Dynamics, Stockport is home of one of the largest lease processing centres in the UK.
